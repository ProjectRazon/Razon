>[!DEFINITION] Definition: Subspace
>
>Let $(V,F,+,\cdot)$ be a [[Vector Space]].
>
>A [[Vector Space]] $(U,F,+,\cdot)$ where $U\subseteq V$ and $\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{u}' \in U$ and $c\mathbf{u}\in U$ for all $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{u}' \in U$ and all $c\in F$.
>

>[!THEOREM] Theorem: Subspace Criterion
>
>A [[Vector Space]] $(U,K,+,\cdot)$ is a [[Subspace]] of $(V,K,+,\cdot)$ if and only if all of the following hold:
>
>1. The zero vector of $V$ is also in $U$;
>2. $\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{u}' \in U \qquad \forall \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{u}' \in U$;
>3. $c\mathbf{u} \in U \qquad \forall c\in F, \mathbf{u} \in U$
>
