# Moras

The [[TODO|phonological]] system of [[index|Japanese]] is based on **moras**, not [[TODO|syllables]]. One can think of spoken Japanese as accompanied by a regular pulse with a constant tempo. Each beat corresponds to exactly one mora. Unlike syllables, which can be either short or long, moras always have the same length. This means that a single syllable can be comprised of one or more moras.

# Orthography

The [[index|Japanese]] language makes use of three different scripts - two [[Writing Systems#Syllabaries|syllabaries]] and one [[Writing Systems#TODO|logographic]] script. The syllabaries, however, do not represent individual syllables. Instead, each character in them represents a single [[Orthography and Phonology#Moras|mora]].

All three scripts are used simultaneously when writing but they usually serve different purposes.

Hiragana is primarily used to represent grammatical elements such as particles, suffixes and inflectional endings. It is also used for certain native Japanese words. Additionally, hiragana can be used as *furigana*, i.e. it may be written alongside [[Orthography and Phonology#Kanji|kanji]] to indicate their pronunciation, especially in learning materials.

# Phonology

## Pitch Accent (È´ò‰Ωé„Ç¢„ÇØ„Çª„É≥„Éà, k≈çtei akusento)

[[index|Japanese]] has a pitch accent system. Each [[Orthography and Phonology#Moras|mora]] has a tone which can be either low or high. The first two moras of each word always have different tones. Throughout a word, if the tone drops to low on some mora, then it will also remain low on the subsequent moras of the word.
